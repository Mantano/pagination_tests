ò r

The "location is everything" rule exempts the convivial Italian wine bar Uvarara, plunked between two cemeteries on a verdant stretch of Metropolitan Avenue. Housed in a former showroom for headstones, the interior is sultry and sexy now, with low, flickering candles, mismatched chairs, and curtains beaded with wine corks. Somehow the locale is perfect, because this kind of cozy bohemian charmer–with a wine list that gets more focused each year and mamma Rosa’s deliciously honest cooking–wouldn't be nearly as unexpected and cool anywhere else.

The menu shifts with the seasons, but mamma is sure to prepare gnocchi alla Romana as well as Neapolitan pizzette, with a decidedly Roman edge. Pay attention to the server’s list of irresistible daily specials.
